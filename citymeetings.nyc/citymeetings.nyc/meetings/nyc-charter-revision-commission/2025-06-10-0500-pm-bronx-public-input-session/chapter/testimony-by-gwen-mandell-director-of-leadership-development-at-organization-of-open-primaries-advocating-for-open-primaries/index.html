Testimony by Gwen Mandell, Director of Leadership Development at Organization of Open Primaries, advocating for open primaries

2:13:39

·

170 sec

Gwen Mandell, Director of Leadership Development at the Organization of Open Primaries, testifies in strong support of open primaries in New York City.

She argues that the current closed system prevents over a million independent voters, including a large proportion of younger voters and voters of color, from participating in the most critical round of voting. Mandell emphasizes that independents choose their status for valid reasons and should not be forced to join a party to vote in taxpayer-funded elections, advocating for a simple change to let all independents vote.

  • Over 1 million NYC voters are prevented from voting in primaries because they are registered as independent.
  • A significant portion of these independent voters are young people and people of color.
  • Mandell argues that forcing independents to join a party to vote is undemocratic and disrespectful to their choice.
  • Advocates for a straightforward open primary system to allow all registered voters to participate.
