Testimony by Eric Budd, Co-founder of People for Voter Turnout, on the benefits of even-year municipal elections

0:48:20

·

3 min

Eric Budd, Co-founder of People for Voter Turnout from Boulder, Colorado, testifies in support of moving municipal elections to even-numbered years.

He shares his experience leading a successful campaign in Boulder to align local elections with state and federal elections, emphasizing that this is the single most effective way to increase voter turnout and ensure more equitable representation, particularly for marginalized demographics whose voices are often diminished in off-cycle elections. Budd also notes that off-cycle elections tend to overrepresent homeowners, which can negatively affect housing policy.

  • Moving municipal elections to align with state/federal elections is the most effective way to boost voter turnout.
  • Off-cycle elections disproportionately reduce turnout among people of color, young people, students, and renters.
  • Such elections tend to overrepresent homeowners, potentially impacting housing policy decisions.
  • Longer ballots in on-cycle elections generally benefit turnout, and the increase in overall voters outweighs marginal increases in down-ballot drop-off.
