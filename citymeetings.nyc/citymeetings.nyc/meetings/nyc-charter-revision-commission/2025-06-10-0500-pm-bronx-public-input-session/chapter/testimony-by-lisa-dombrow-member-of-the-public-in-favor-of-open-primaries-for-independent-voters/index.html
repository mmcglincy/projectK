Testimony by Lisa Dombrow, Member of the Public, in favor of open primaries for independent voters

2:19:27

·

139 sec

Lisa Dombrow, a Brooklyn artist, educator, and long-time independent voter, testifies in support of open primaries.

She explains her choice to be independent stems from a belief that no single party fully represents her views and that major parties prioritize power over problem-solving. Dombrow argues it is unfair and undemocratic to be locked out of taxpayer-funded primary elections, which are often decisive in New York City, and that forcing independents to register with a party negates their political identity.

  • Dombrow is an independent voter because she feels no party fully represents her and believes parties prioritize power over solutions.
  • She argues that being excluded from primaries is unfair, as these elections are taxpayer-funded and often determine the outcome in NYC.
  • Forcing independents to join a party to vote is seen as disrespectful to their political convictions.
