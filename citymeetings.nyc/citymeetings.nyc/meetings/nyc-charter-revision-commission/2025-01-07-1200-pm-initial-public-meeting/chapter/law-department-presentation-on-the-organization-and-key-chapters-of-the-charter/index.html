Law Department presentation on the organization and key chapters of the Charter

1:05:32

·

4 min

Brenda Cooke outlines the organization of the NYC Charter into chapters.

She explains that early chapters detail the powers and duties of elected officials (Mayor, Council, Borough Presidents, Comptroller), followed by chapters covering fundamental government processes like budgeting, city planning, and procurement. Cooke notes there are over 40 chapters dedicated to specific city agencies and highlights several key chapters governing processes like administrative rulemaking (CAPA), elections, open meetings, term limits, conflicts of interest, and community district structures.

  • Chapters cover elected officials, key government processes (budget, planning, procurement), and specific agencies.
  • Key process chapters include: Ch 45 (Rulemaking/CAPA), Ch 46 (Elections), Ch 47 (Open Meetings), Ch 50/50A (Term Limits), Ch 68 (Conflicts of Interest), Ch 69 (Community Districts/Coterminality).
  • Coterminality (aligning service districts) was a focus of the 1975 commission.
