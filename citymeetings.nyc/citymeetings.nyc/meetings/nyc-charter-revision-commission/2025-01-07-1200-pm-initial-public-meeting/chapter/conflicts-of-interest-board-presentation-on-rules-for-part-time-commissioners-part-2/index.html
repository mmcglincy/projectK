Conflicts of Interest Board presentation on rules for part-time commissioners, part 2

0:42:38

·

3 min

Alex Kipp continues the COIB presentation, outlining rules specific to part-time commissioners.

He explains that restrictions on making paid communications on behalf of private parties only apply to communications directed to the Charter Revision Commission itself. Kipp details the city's $50 valuable gift rule but highlights the "social exception" for gifts clearly stemming from pre-existing personal or professional relationships unrelated to commission work, while advising caution if the gift giver has interests before the commission.

  • Commissioners cannot make paid communications to the CRC on behalf of private parties without a waiver.
  • The general rule prohibits accepting valuable gifts ($50+) from those doing business with the city.
  • A "social exception" allows accepting gifts where the clear motivation is a pre-existing relationship (e.g., employer holiday party, family).
  • Caution and seeking advice are recommended if a gift giver also has specific matters before the commission.
  • Gifts offered because of one's status as a commissioner should be refused.
