Law Department presentation on the NYC Charter's structure and legal context

0:56:53

·

3 min

Brenda Cooke from the NYC Law Department's Legal Counsel Division introduces herself and her colleague Ben Miller, offering their division's support to the commission.

She provides an overview of the NYC Charter, describing it as a foundational document outlining the structure, powers, and responsibilities of city government. Cooke explains it's a "short form" charter focusing on general authority, with more detailed operational rules found in the Administrative Code and agency regulations, and notes its legal authority derives from New York State law, particularly the Municipal Home Rule Law.

  • The Law Department's Legal Counsel Division will assist the commission.
  • The Charter sets out the general structure, powers, and responsibilities of NYC government.
  • It's a "short form" document; details are often in the Admin Code or agency rules.
  • NYC government authority derives from the NYS Constitution and state laws (e.g., Municipal Home Rule Law).
