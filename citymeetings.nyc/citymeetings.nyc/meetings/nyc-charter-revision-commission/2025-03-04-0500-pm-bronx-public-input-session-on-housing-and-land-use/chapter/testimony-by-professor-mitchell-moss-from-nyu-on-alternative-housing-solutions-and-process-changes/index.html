TESTIMONY

Testimony by Professor Mitchell Moss from NYU on alternative housing solutions and process changes

1:50:10

·

7 min

Professor Mitchell Moss from NYU argues that blaming only ULURP for stalled housing is incomplete, citing over-zealous landmarking and inflexible zoning as contributing factors.

He proposes several charter changes: empowering Borough Presidents by giving them expedited review authority over small residential projects (8 units or less) bypassing ULURP; requiring 10-year infrastructure impact assessments for projects; and updating building codes to reflect modern technology and lifestyles by eliminating requirements for windows in every bedroom (allowing office conversions) and mandatory baking ovens in kitchens (reducing unit costs).

  • Moss argues landmarking and restrictive zoning contribute to housing shortage alongside ULURP.
  • Proposes expedited (60-day) Borough President review/approval for small residential projects (<=8 units), bypassing ULURP.
  • Suggests requiring 10-year environmental impact assessments for projects (water, waste, transit).
  • Advocates eliminating window requirements for bedrooms in conversions, relying on modern ventilation.
  • Recommends removing mandatory baking ovens from kitchens and allowing shared kitchens to lower housing costs.
