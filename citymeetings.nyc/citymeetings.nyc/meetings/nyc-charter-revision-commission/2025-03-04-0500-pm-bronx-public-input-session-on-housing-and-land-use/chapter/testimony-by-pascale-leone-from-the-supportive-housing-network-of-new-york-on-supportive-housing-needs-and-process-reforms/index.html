TESTIMONY

Testimony by Pascale Leone from the Supportive Housing Network of New York on supportive housing needs and process reforms

1:09:40

·

6 min

Pascale Leone, Executive Director of the Supportive Housing Network of New York, emphasizes the critical need for supportive housing (affordable housing with services) to combat rising homelessness.

She criticizes the current land use process as too slow (3-5 years before construction) and failing to account for the costs of inaction, advocating for faster approvals for supportive and affordable projects and better inclusion of marginalized voices.

Leone also highlights severe problems with city procurement, where late contract registrations and payments cripple nonprofit providers, recommending mandated agency timelines and interest penalties for late payments.

  • NYC faces record homelessness, increasing the need for supportive housing.
  • Supportive housing provides permanent homes and services for individuals with significant barriers like mental illness or substance use disorder.
  • Current land use review is too slow (ULURP taking 1-2 years plus pre-certification delays) and site-focused.
  • Leone recommends fast-tracking 100% affordable/supportive projects and improving agency coordination.
  • Chronic delays in city contract registration and payments force nonprofits to rely on costly lines of credit, jeopardizing services.
