Testimony by Raquel Centeno, Postdoctoral Scholar at Caltech Center for Science, Society, and Policy, on primary systems and voter turnout

0:10:45

·

168 sec

Raquel Centeno, a postdoctoral scholar at the Caltech Center for Science, Society, and Policy, presents research on the demobilizing effect of primary electoral institutions on Asian American and Latino voters.

Her research indicates that closed partisan primaries are associated with lower general election turnout for all groups, but particularly impact Asian American and Latino voters who identify as independents at higher rates.

She suggests that moving from closed primaries to systems like open primaries or top-two/top-four primaries could lead to positive gains in turnout for these communities.
