TESTIMONY

Testimony by Steve Levin, CEO of Solar One, on expanding solar energy access in NYC

0:31:24

·

5 min

Steve Levin emphasizes the critical role of solar energy, particularly community solar, in meeting New York City's climate goals, reducing reliance on peaker plants, and providing affordable, clean energy access to all residents, especially low-income communities.

He notes that lack of space is a major barrier to community solar expansion and advocates for leveraging city-owned property and streamlining processes, specifically urging the commission to make it easier for the New York Power Authority (NYPA) to partner with the city on public renewable projects.

  • NYC has ambitious goals for carbon neutrality, solar power, and energy storage deployment.
  • Community solar is key for equitable access, providing savings for renters and those with unsuitable roofs.
  • Lack of space hinders community solar growth needed for programs like Statewide Solar For All and NYPA's REACH.
  • Leveraging city assets and facilitating partnerships with NYPA are crucial for building publicly owned renewables.
