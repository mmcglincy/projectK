TESTIMONY

Testimony by Elijah Hutchinson, Executive Director of MOCEJ, on the climate crisis and ULURP challenges

0:13:07

·

6 min

Elijah Hutchinson outlines the severe climate threats facing New York City, including flooding and extreme heat, emphasizing the disproportionate impact on vulnerable communities and the economic costs.

He argues for scaling up climate infrastructure but identifies the current ULURP process as a significant barrier to projects involving streets, sidewalks, waterfront areas, and city-owned property needed for resilience measures like flood protection and solar installations.

Hutchinson requests charter revisions to expedite these processes, particularly concerning small property acquisitions, street grade changes, work on mapped streets, and site selection for climate infrastructure.

  • NYC faces significant threats from sea-level rise, increased rainfall, and extreme heat, with major economic and health costs.
  • Investing in resilience infrastructure offers substantial returns by avoiding future losses.
  • ULURP requirements complicate and delay projects involving property acquisition, street modifications, waterfront access, and installing solar on city property.
  • Streets and sidewalks are crucial "climate corridors" but current processes hinder their adaptation.
  • Streamlining ULURP for specific climate-related actions is needed to accelerate resilience efforts.
