TESTIMONY

Testimony by Council Member Lincoln Restler on the Charter Revision Commission's scope, balance of power, and election reform proposals

0:25:04

·

5 min

Council Member Lincoln Restler expresses concerns about the Charter Revision Commission's origins, scope, and perceived lack of connection to elected leadership. He questions whether the commission is overstepping its bounds by addressing a wide range of topics beyond its initial housing focus, potentially acting as a legislative body rather than focusing on governmental structure and process.

Restler voices deep concern that the preliminary report's proposals appear to uniformly increase mayoral power while limiting council and other elected officials' power, especially troubling given current mayoral controversies. He urges the commission to reconsider its broad approach and focus on housing, particularly streamlining the ULURP process to address the housing crisis, and suggests giving ranked-choice voting more time before major revisions.
