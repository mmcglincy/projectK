New York City Council

Committee on Public Housing

Mar 10, 2025

·

10:30 AM

3 hr 31 min

Summary

A hearing on NYCHA's fiscal year 2026 preliminary budget and five-year operating and capital plans.

Discussions focused on NYCHA's $545 million rent arrears, $80 billion capital needs backlog, implementation of new rental assistance programs, and the controversial RAD/PACT program.

Council members expressed concerns about maintenance issues, repair backlogs, and slow response times despite city funding. NYCHA officials cited federal underfunding as a major cause of ongoing problems while highlighting progress in areas like lead abatement and mold reduction.

Residents and advocates called for increased capital funding, more resident input in decision-making, and alternatives to RAD/PACT conversions.

Features testimony from NYCHA officials, including CEO Lisa Bova-Hiatt and CFO Annika Lescott-Martinez, and the public.
