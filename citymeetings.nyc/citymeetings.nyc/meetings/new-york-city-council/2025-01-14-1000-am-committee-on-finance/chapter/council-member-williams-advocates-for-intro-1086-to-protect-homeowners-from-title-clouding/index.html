Council Member Williams advocates for Intro 1086 to protect homeowners from title clouding

0:13:14

·

3 min

Council Member Nantasha M. Williams presents and advocates for Intro 1086, a bill designed to address the issue of title clouding and protect homeowners from fraud and property loss. The proposed legislation would require the Department of Finance to notify property owners within 30 days when deed-related or mortgage-related documents are recorded for their property.

  • The bill aims to enhance transparency and create additional safeguards against title clouding.
  • Notifications would be sent via email, text message, and postal mail to ensure property owners are informed through multiple channels.
  • Williams emphasizes that title clouding disproportionately affects low-income and elderly homeowners, making this legislation particularly important for vulnerable populations.
