PUBLIC TESTIMONY

Testimony by Adama Bah, Founder and Executive Director of Afrikana, on Resources for Immigrant Youth

0:39:26

·

4 min

Adama Bah, Founder and Executive Director of Afrikana, testified about the urgent need for better support and resources for unaccompanied immigrant minors in New York City. She highlighted the vulnerabilities these children face, including risks of trafficking, assault, and mental health issues, and called for immediate action from the city government.

  • Emphasized the need for culturally competent and language-appropriate services
  • Requested the creation of a 24-hour DYCD drop-in center for unaccompanied minors
  • Expressed concerns about contacting embassies for documents, which could put children at risk
  • Highlighted the issue of age verification and the need for more beds for 18-24 year olds in DYCD facilities
