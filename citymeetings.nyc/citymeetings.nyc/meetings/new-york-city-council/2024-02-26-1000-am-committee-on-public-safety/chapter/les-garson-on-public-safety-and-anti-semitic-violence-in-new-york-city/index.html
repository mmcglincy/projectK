Les Garson on Public Safety and Anti-Semitic Violence in New York City

2:13:45

·

4 min

Les Garson speaks about feeling unsafe in New York City due to anti-Semitic violence and protests following the Hamas attacks on Israel. She shares personal experiences of fear and lack of security in public spaces and emphasizes the need for stronger law enforcement to protect citizens from hate-inspired violence. Her testimony connects the rise in public demonstrations celebrating the attacks on Israel and calling for genocide to his increased personal sense of danger. Garson advocates for empowering the police to enforce laws against unlawful protests and violence, aiming to safeguard all residents, regardless of religion, from disruptive behavior and violence.
