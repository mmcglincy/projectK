Council Member Restler advocates for school libraries and librarians

0:12:41

·

141 sec

Council Member Lincoln Restler passionately addresses the critical shortage of school libraries and librarians in New York City public schools. He emphasizes the importance of libraries for student learning and literacy, and discusses the proposed legislation Intro 1125 to improve data collection on school libraries.

  • Restler highlights that only 16% of NYC schools have a librarian, and 30% (480 schools) don't have a library at all.
  • He argues that the lack of libraries is inconsistent with the mayor's stated focus on literacy.
  • Intro 1125 is presented as a solution to gather real-time data on school libraries and librarians, which could help address the issue.
