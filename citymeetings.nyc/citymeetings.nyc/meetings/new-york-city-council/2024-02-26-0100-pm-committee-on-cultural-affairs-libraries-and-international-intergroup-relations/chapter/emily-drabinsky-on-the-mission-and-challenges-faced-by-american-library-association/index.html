Emily Drabinsky on the Mission and Challenges Faced by American Library Association

1:05:42

·

3 min

Emily Drabinsky, President of the American Library Association, provides detailed remarks on the mission and challenges faced by the association. She highlights the foundational mission of ALA to enhance learning and ensure access to all, emphasizing inclusivity. Drabinsky indicates the crucial role of library workers and the necessity for their autonomy and authority to be respected for intellectual freedom and equitable access. She describes the alarming increase in attempts to restrict access to books and programming, especially those reflecting the lives of marginalized communities, viewing such efforts as contrary to the ALA’s goal of building broad collections for public benefit. Drabinsky advocates for adequate funding for libraries to combat book banning and public funding cuts, recalling ALA's history of defending reading freedom, including the 'United Against Book Bans' campaign. She concludes with a call for partnerships in promoting reading access and reaffirms ALA’s commitment against any form of reading restriction.
