PUBLIC TESTIMONY

Testimony by Rob Bookman, Counsel for New York City Hospitality Alliance, on NYC's Outdoor Dining Program

0:18:40

·

8 min

Rob Bookman, counsel for the New York City Hospitality Alliance, provided testimony on the challenges and shortcomings of the new permanent outdoor dining program in NYC. He emphasized that despite good intentions, the program has not been successful due to various issues in legislation, rulemaking, and implementation.

  • Low participation rates: Only about 3,000 establishments applied, far fewer than expected
  • Key problems: stricter clearance requirements, high expenses, seasonal nature of roadway dining, complex online-only application process, and the continuation of the consent process
  • Suggestions for improvement: policy shifts (e.g., changing processing time interpretation), rule changes, and potential statutory changes to create a better balance between the old restrictive law and the permissive emergency program
