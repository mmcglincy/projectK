TESTIMONY

Kim Darga on the J51 Tax Incentive Program

0:05:50

·

11 min

Kim Darga, Deputy Commissioner, Development, Department of Housing Preservation and Development, provides an extensive testimony on the J51 Tax Incentive Program. Darga explains the program’s objectives, historical context, the proposed changes, and the rationale behind these transformations. The testimony addresses the program's focus on housing quality, stabilization, and efforts to incentivize major capital improvements in low-cost residential buildings. The discourse includes the program’s evolution, emphasizing the shift towards a more targeted approach aimed at buildings housing lower-income New Yorkers and explaining the program's impact on rent-stabilized properties. Key components such as eligibility criteria, enforcement mechanisms, and anticipated benefits towards sustainability and affordability are outlined. Darga provides detailed responses to questions on program administration, including compliance, the characteristics of buildings that have benefited, and procedural aspects of applying for and receiving J51 benefits. The testimony concludes with Council Member Pierina Ana Sanchez transitioning the discussion to Council Member Alexa Avilés for further inquiries.
