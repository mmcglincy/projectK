Can successful school programs be duplicated in nearby schools to utilize available space?

1:56:16

153 sec

The Department of Education discusses efforts to replicate successful programs in under-enrolled schools to attract families and face challenges in altering parent preferences for certain prestigious schools.

  • Superintendents are currently engaging in efforts to leverage under-enrolled schools by duplicating successful programs.
  • Despite initiatives to promote less recognized but excellent school programs, parent preferences for prestigious schools remain a significant challenge.
  • The Department of Education, including the Chancellor, is actively working to highlight these programs through various channels, including social media.
  • Council Member Rita C. Joseph emphasizes the importance of publicizing successful yet under-recognized programs within the public school system, like the performing arts school in Brooklyn.
