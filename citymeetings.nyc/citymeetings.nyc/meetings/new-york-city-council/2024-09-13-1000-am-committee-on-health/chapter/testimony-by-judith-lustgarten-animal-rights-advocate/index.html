PUBLIC TESTIMONY

Testimony by Judith Lustgarten, Animal Rights Advocate

7:13:02

·

5 min

Judith Lustgarten, an animal rights advocate, provided critical testimony on the state of animal welfare in New York City.

She highlighted the lack of proper oversight, insufficient resources, and questionable practices at ACC, calling for significant improvements in the management and operation of animal services in the city.

  • Criticized the lack of a formal city committee for animal welfare and the ineffectiveness of the existing animal welfare agency
  • Pointed out the absence of marketing efforts by ACC, leading to low public awareness and adoption rates
  • Raised concerns about the conditions in ACC shelters, including cleanliness issues and inappropriate use of medications
  • Called for the implementation of proper business protocols and the establishment of a civilian review board to improve animal welfare services
