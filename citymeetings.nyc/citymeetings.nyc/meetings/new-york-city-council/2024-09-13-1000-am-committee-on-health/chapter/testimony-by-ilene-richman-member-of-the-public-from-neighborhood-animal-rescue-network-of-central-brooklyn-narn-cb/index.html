PUBLIC TESTIMONY

Testimony by Ilene Richman, Member of the Public from Neighborhood Animal Rescue Network of Central Brooklyn (NARN-CB)

4:18:22

·

115 sec

Ilene Richman, a member of the Neighborhood Animal Rescue Network of Central Brooklyn (NARN-CB), testified about the animal welfare challenges in District 36, particularly focusing on the large population of street cats. She highlighted the connection between animal welfare issues and community inequity, and called for increased city investment in animal welfare services.

  • Richman founded NARN-CB, a collective of 75 members working on animal rescue in Central Brooklyn
  • She emphasized the need for free spay/neuter services and affordable veterinary care in underserved neighborhoods
  • Richman stressed that community self-help efforts are not enough, and more city investment in animal welfare is necessary
