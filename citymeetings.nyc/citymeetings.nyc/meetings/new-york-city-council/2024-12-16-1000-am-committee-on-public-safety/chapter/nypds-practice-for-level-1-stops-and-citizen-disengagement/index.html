NYPD's practice for level 1 stops and citizen disengagement

2:41:27

·

83 sec

Council Member Yousef Salaam inquires about NYPD's practices when individuals attempt to disengage from a level 1 stop. NYPD officials explain that citizens are free to leave during a level 1 encounter and clarify when such an encounter becomes a level 3 stop.

  • Officials emphasize that preventing someone from leaving during a level 1 encounter is inappropriate and would constitute an improper Terry stop.
  • The discussion highlights the importance of citizens' rights during police encounters.
  • NYPD representatives stress the need for officers to respect individuals' freedom of movement during level 1 encounters.
