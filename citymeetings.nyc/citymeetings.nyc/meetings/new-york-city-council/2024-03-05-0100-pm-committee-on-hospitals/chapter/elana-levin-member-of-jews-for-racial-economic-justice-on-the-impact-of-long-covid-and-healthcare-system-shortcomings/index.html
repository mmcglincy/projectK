Elana Levin, Member of Jews For Racial & Economic Justice, on the Impact of Long COVID and Healthcare System Shortcomings

2:31:33

·

161 sec

Elana Levin shares her struggle with long COVID and points out flaws in NYC's healthcare system.

  • Levin contracted long COVID in December 2022 despite taking all precautionary measures, highlighting the unpredictable nature of the illness.
  • She criticizes the discontinuation of free COVID testing and changes to mask mandates in healthcare settings, emphasizing the heightened risk of virus spread.
  • Levin discusses her difficulty in accessing safe medical care due to the lack of effective masks and accommodations for the immune-compromised.
  • She draws parallels between the current handling of the COVID crisis and the early days of the AIDS crisis, suggesting a lack of adequate response.
  • Levin advocates for practical measures such as improved mask distribution, air filtration enhancements, and stricter healthcare protocols to mitigate further COVID transmission.
