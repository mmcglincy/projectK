Prom Freedman on Addressing Systemic Injustices through Closing Rikers and Reparations

5:46:54

·

3 min

Prom Freedman, an organizer, reparationist, and American Freedman citizen, emphasizes the importance of closing Rikers Island and offering reparations and support for oppressed communities.

  • Freedman voices the perspective of descendants of those emancipated by the 13th Amendment, advocating for justice reform and reparations.
  • Highlights the negative impact of a $1,000,000,000 budget intended to incarcerate and oppress communities in Bronx and Harlem, areas Freedman is personally connected to.
  • Calls for the closure of Rikers Island and the implementation of reparations for American Freedman, alongside prioritizing youth and family opportunities.
  • Criticizes the city council's handling of questions and the discrimination against formerly incarcerated individuals, urging action on legal and moral grounds.
