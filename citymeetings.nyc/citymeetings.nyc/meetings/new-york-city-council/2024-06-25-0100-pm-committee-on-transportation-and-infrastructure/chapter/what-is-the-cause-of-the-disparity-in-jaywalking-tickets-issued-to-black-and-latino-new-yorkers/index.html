QUESTION

What is the cause of the disparity in jaywalking tickets issued to Black and Latino New Yorkers?

0:58:05

·

11 min

Deputy Chief Thomas Alps acknowledges the data showing a disproportionate issuance of jaywalking tickets to Black and Latino communities.

  • He explains that an analysis found jaywalking summonses are not commonly issued citywide, with 23 of 77 police precincts not issuing any.
  • Alps states the 115th Precinct issued the most at 45 summonses year-to-date, which he attributes to data-driven 'precision policing' in areas with high pedestrian fatalities.
  • He maintains the 115th Precinct conducted significant educational outreach on traffic safety prior to enforcement.
  • Alps defends the use of jaywalking summonses as necessary for ensuring predictable behavior and safety among all modes of transportation.
