Connie Bordenga from the American Cancer Society testifies in strong support of Memorial Sloan Kettering's (MSK) proposed Pavilion project, highlighting the organization's close partnership with MSK and the critical need for expanded cancer care facilities in New York City.

  • The American Cancer Society currently funds over $3.7 million in research grants at MSK and collaborates on various cancer prevention and support initiatives.
  • MSK is described as the largest referral source for the American Cancer Society's free lodging program for cancer patients during treatment.
  • The proposed Pavilion, with 28 operating rooms and additional patient beds, is deemed necessary to meet the rising cancer rates and healthcare needs of New Yorkers.
