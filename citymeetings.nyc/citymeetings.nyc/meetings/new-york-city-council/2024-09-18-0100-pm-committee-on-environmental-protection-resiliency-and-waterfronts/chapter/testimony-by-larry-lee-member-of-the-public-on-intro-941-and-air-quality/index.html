PUBLIC TESTIMONY

Testimony by Larry Lee, Member of the Public, on Intro 941 and Air Quality

3:04:03

·

3 min

Larry Lee, a lifelong New Yorker, testifies against Intro 941, focusing on the proposed 5-day deadline for submitting complaints in the Citizens Air Complaint Program. He argues that this deadline is unreasonably aggressive and suggests that the Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) should focus on fixing its processes instead.

  • Lee shares a personal story about his friend Manny Ho, who died of lung cancer despite a healthy lifestyle, attributing his death to air pollution in the East Village.
  • He emphasizes the simplicity of turning off a vehicle's engine to prevent idling, using his mother's words: "Now was that that hard?"
  • The testimony highlights the health impacts of air pollution and the importance of the citizen complaint program in addressing idling vehicles.
