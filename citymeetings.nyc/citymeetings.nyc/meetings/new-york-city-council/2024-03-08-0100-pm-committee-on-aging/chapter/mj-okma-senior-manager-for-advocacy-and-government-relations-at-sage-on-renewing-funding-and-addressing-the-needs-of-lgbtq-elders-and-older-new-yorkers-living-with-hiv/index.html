138 sec

MJ Okma highlights the vital services provided by SAGE for LGBTQ+ elders and older New Yorkers living with HIV, stressing the need for renewed funding and increased investment in aging services.

  • Okma emphasizes the significant role SAGE has played over four decades in offering comprehensive social services and community programs to LGBTQ+ older adults and those living with HIV.
  • They point out the critical situation of LGBTQ+ elders being invisible, under-served, and isolated, with a rapidly growing population amidst insufficient support.
  • In fiscal year 25, SAGE seeks to renew its funding to continue vital initiatives, like the LGBTQIA Older Adult Centers in various boroughs.
  • Okma warns of the detrimental impacts that funding cuts to aging services could have on these communities, given the systematic barriers they face.
  • They underscore the necessity for more investments in NYC Aging, the city's sole agency for older individuals, to better meet the needs of diverse and growing older populations.
