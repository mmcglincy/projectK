QUESTION

How are private social adult care centers impacting NYC Aging's older adult centers?

0:34:45

·

3 min

NYC Department for the Aging registers private SADCs annually, and the city imposes higher fees for late registration to ensure compliance and generate revenue.

  • NYC Aging does not have direct oversight of private SADC programs, but under Local Law Nine of 2015, programs must register with NYC Aging.
  • Council Member Adrienne Adams raises concerns about the growth of private SADCs and their impact on NYC Aging's older adult centers' attendance.
  • Lorraine Cortes-Vasquez, the Commissioner, mentions efforts to rigorously register SADCs and impose higher fees for non-compliance.
  • The exact number of registered SADCs and detailed data on fines and investigations are to be provided later.
