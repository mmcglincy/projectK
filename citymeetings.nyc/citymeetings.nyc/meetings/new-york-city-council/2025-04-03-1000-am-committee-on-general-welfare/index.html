New York City Council

Committee on General Welfare

Apr 03, 2025

·

10:00 AM

4 hr 5 min

Summary

A hearing on hunger and food insecurity in New York City, discussing current challenges and potential solutions.

The committee examined the Community Food Connection program funding, impacts of potential federal cuts to nutrition programs, and increasing demand at food banks and pantries.

Three bills were discussed:

  • Creating a universal benefits application
  • Automatically enrolling individuals in city-created benefits programs
  • Requiring receipts for benefit applications

Discussions focused on improving access to food assistance programs, especially for vulnerable populations, and the need for increased city investment in food security initiatives.

Features testimony from the Department of Social Services (DSS), Human Resources Administration (HRA), and the public.
