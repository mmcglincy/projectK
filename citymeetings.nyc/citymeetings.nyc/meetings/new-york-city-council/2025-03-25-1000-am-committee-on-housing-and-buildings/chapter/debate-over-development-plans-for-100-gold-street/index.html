Council Member Brewer inquires about the possibility of making the development at 100 Gold Street a community land trust with 100% affordable housing. Commissioner Tigani explains the current plans and constraints.

  • The site is currently subject to an RFP (Request for Proposals)
  • The project aims to deliver significant affordable housing in lower Manhattan
  • The current building needs $230 million in capital repairs
  • The project also aims to provide modern office space for city agencies
  • A community land trust proposal is not precluded, but needs to balance multiple objectives
